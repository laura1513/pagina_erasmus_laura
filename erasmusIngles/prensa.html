The Erasmus+ program holds its first information meeting

The Dos Hermanas City Council, through the Delegation of Economic Promotion and Innovation, recently held the first information meeting with students selected to participate in the Erasmus+ internship program abroad.

The selected students, from Dos Hermanas Vocational Training educational centers, will carry out their Training practices at the Work Center lasting 90 days in several countries of the European Union. On this occasion, the countries that will receive the students from Dos Hermanas are Italy, Germany or Portugal, among others.

The IES Miguel de Molinos in Zaragoza begins its Erasmus+ journey

The IES Miguel de Molinos in Zaragoza has joined the Erasmus+ adventure this year. After having achieved accreditation of the program, the institute has launched the first project called Windows to the future.

Throughout this course, different mobilities of teachers and students will be organized in order to continue working on educational innovation, education where everyone has a place, the internationalization of the center, and dynamic and responsible learning.

“We have had the opportunity to observe how Sweden works, to learn about an educational system that is one of the most innovative in Europe and that invests the most in education (most of the centers are financed by municipalities, and they allocate up to 40% of their budget to education)”, they explain from the center.
